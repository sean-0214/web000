Risk Measurement Methodologies

The system implements multiple risk measurement approaches to provide comprehensive risk assessment:

Value at Risk (VaR) Implementation:

  • Historical Simulation: Non-parametric approach using historical returns
  • Parametric VaR: Normal distribution assumption with volatility modeling
  • Monte Carlo Simulation: Stochastic modeling for complex portfolios
  • GARCH Models: Time-varying volatility estimation
  • Extreme Value Theory: Tail risk modeling for extreme events

Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR):

  • Expected Shortfall: Average loss beyond VaR threshold
  • Coherent Risk Measure: Mathematically consistent risk metric
  • Optimization Integration: CVaR-based portfolio optimization
  • Backtesting Framework: Historical performance validation
  • Regulatory Compliance: Basel III and regulatory requirements

Stress Testing Framework

Comprehensive stress testing capabilities to assess portfolio resilience under adverse scenarios:

Scenario Analysis:

  • Historical Scenarios: Replication of past market crises
  • Hypothetical Scenarios: Custom stress scenarios
  • Monte Carlo Stress Testing: Probabilistic scenario generation
  • Factor Shock Analysis: Individual risk factor stress testing
  • Correlation Breakdown: Stress testing correlation assumptions

Regulatory Stress Tests:

  • CCAR (Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review) scenarios
  • EBA (European Banking Authority) stress test scenarios
  • HKMA (Hong Kong Monetary Authority) stress testing guidelines
  • Custom regulatory scenario implementation
  • Automated regulatory reporting capabilities

Portfolio Optimization Integration

Optimization Algorithms:
